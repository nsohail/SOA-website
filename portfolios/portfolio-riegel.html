About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (Graphic Design)

Minor History

Degree BA Art and Visual Technology

Painting, illustration, drawing, and printmaking are all areas that I have taken upon myself to learn as a way to realize a breadth of experiences that will add value to my end goal as a graphic designer. However it isn’t enough to dabble and experiment in all these mediums. I am following graphic design both from the perspective of its practicality and in the belief that one can add his own impact on a growing part of this profession as well as leave an impact on society as a whole. And in the aspect of society it is the ability of the graphic designer to convey information with a clarity of message and/or intent. Nonetheless this does not have to limit my creative self and my desire to create a graphic of my own. Innovation in a product, for example, requires a level of creativity to expand its respective market. However it is a process that requires planning and collaboration. Its source, the idea can be a very personal one. It could be someone’s dream. And I find some comfort in the idea that I am making a small effort and have it affect in a big way, like being a part of something greater even if it’s superficial. There is a creative process in this collaboration, and I want to be a part of that creative process. The book Manual for Recruiting and Function of the V.N. mirrors the wonder in reimagining this world for me. It is the play on the idea of a ‘what-if scenario.’ What if there was a fictional clandestine organization, whose goal it was to understand the absurdity in their world? Absurdities that are filled with wonder that goes beyond our understanding and makes us rethink the world at large. What if you were newly inducted into that organization? You would need an employee’s handbook wouldn’t you? That is the essence of this book. There are goals and protocols that you need to follow. And finally there are the objects that they contain—that an employee must understand. Between the clandestine organization and the objects, there is this rethinking and reshaping of the world that can enrich our lives. Although it is fictional, it can help to impart to the reader a way to creatively rethink their own world. Even the humblest of objects can be interesting. And thus if they can tap into that creativity in the way they view things in our world, then they can tap into that creativity anywhere
