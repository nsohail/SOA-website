About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (Graphic Design)

Degree BA Art and Visual Technology

I love design and I love to draw. My ultimate dream is to design book covers and/or illustrate children’s books. Everywhere I go I see design and all of its elements. I see typefaces, grids, and color schemes. Graphic design is an art that uses skill, information, and technology to communicate to the world. Becoming a graphic designer is something that I have grown to enjoy from creating logos, to business cards, to posters. Being a designer requires you to have the talent and style as well as determination and commitment. I feel that there’s more to a graphic designer than just skill. You need to be able to focus on your goal and be able to put forth the time and effort to reach them within deadlines. Many skills are needed in order to be a graphic designer. Being organized and having time management helps me tremendously. Knowing and managing my time or setting deadlines has improved my judgment and decision-making. It’s important that I have a visual sensibility to understand what works and what doesn’t work. Graphic designers play a significant role in the media today. Everyday objects, from billboards to signs, to websites and cereal boxes are all created by a graphic designer. Graphic designers hold the responsibility to create visuals that help communicate information, events, and items.

The Adventures of Koda Bear is a children’s coloring book with three stories of obedience, loyalty, and love. My intended audience is young children, pre-school to fifth grade. Young children with creative, artistic minds will enjoy this unique book. While the children read this story they can illustrate it by coloring the pages that go along with the story being told. The goal of this coloring book is to make kids want to read it all the way through while learning, coloring, and completing activities at the same time. I hope to achieve a playful tone while teaching the children life lessons at the same time. My own puppy, Koda, and a little girl named McKenzie, inspired this book. This book is dedicated to them.
