student name: Kimberly Dunnmajor: Art and Visual Technology (Graphic Design) degree: BA Art and Visual Technology minor: Business There are several things that make up and define me, so I will start with a few. I was born in Texas and lived there until I was eighteen. It is where I spent most of my life up until this point, and will always be what I call home. My father is a retired Air Force veteran, thus making me a former military brat, andvery good at assimilating to new environments, people, and situations. My husband is also in the Military, so the military lifestyle will always be a part of my life. This means there will always be change, and I am not afraid of change. I am a student; not just in class or on campus but everywhere. I love to learn new things and more importantly, I love to make things. I make art, graphics, soap, candy, clothes, and food; if I can’t find what I am looking for, I make it. I love listening to music, reading books, going to concerts, doing new things, and experiencing what I can whenever possible. My style is bulky, blunt, colorful, and fun. I like making pieces that are exciting and engaging. I also have an affinity for filigree and line art. Solid two-dimensional flat line art is definitely my cup of tea. And at the end of the day I just want to do the best that I can in whichever path I walk down. I want my book to bring light to Hans Christian Anderson’s The Little Mermaid—a story that has been altered beyond recognition several times over. While it has been presented, edited, abridged, and added collections of stories plenty of times, I wanted to single it out and bring it back to life—a sort of revival. I want readers to be engaged in my illustrations to see what I see, and feel what I feel whenreading this story that has been a part of my life before I can even remember. I hope readers are eager to turn pages, and see what Illustrations I’ve come up with to express and convey the contents of the text. My ultimate goal with this book is for the reader to read a traditional story they thought they knew, and turn it into something brand new
