About Me

Design Portfolio

As a designer, I draw inspiration from the world around me—from past experiences as well as hobbies and goals. I was born in Germany, raised in England and spent much of my life moving from place to place. As a result, I love learning about other cultures and trying new cuisines. In high school I developed a love of old black and white films and this introduced me to the jazz era fashion, music and dance. I continue to refer to them as a source of inspiration in my artwork, and I have even become an avid swing dancer. Another passion of mine is teaching. I currently teach drawing to children, but my dream is to one day teach high school level graphic design.

My book, It Don’t Mean A Thing If You Ain’t Got That Swing: A History of Swing Dancing, is inspired by my love of swing dance and vintage design. Swing dance is a broad umbrella term that encompasses many variations of the dance. In my book, I focus on the history behind each form of dance as well as the genre in general. Swing dancing was a cultural phenomenon that was incredibly popular during the 1920s to the 1950s and continues to be danced to this day. It is a product of the times and is directly influenced by the political, social and cultural events happening in the world at the time. My book design is inspired by the Art Deco movement that was so popular during the golden years of swing dance. I hope that my book will help to inform readers about the dance in relation to history and to celebrate the dance that I am so passionate about.
