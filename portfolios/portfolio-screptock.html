Morgan Screptock

About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (Graphic Design)

Minor Communication

Degree BA Art and Visual Technology

As I child, I dreamed of growing up to be a beret wearing, paintbrush wielding artist. I loved to create. When I was in elementary school, I tried to start my own little business making greeting cards. I had no clue what graphic design was, or that I would one day earn a degree in it. This little business, which I excitedly named “Kitty Corps,” was my first exposure to design. In my time studying at George Mason, I’ve developed my own unique design aesthetic. My style is influenced by minimalist design, as well as various aspects of contemporary art and modern digital media. After graduating, I plan to continue my career in the design industry and hope to one day become an art director. For my book, I chose to combine my love for design with my love for cities to create a travel guide for Washington, DC. With destinations geared towards young adults, The Neighborhood Guide to Washington, DC is a travel guide for both tourists and locals alike. Featuring history and photography from some of Washington’s most vibrant neighborhoods, my book shows a side of the nation’s capital that you’ve never seen before. I chose to incorporate a minimalist design aesthetic to let my photography stand out and capture the essence of the city.
