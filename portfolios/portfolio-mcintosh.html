Lael McIntosh

About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (Graphic Design)

Degree BA Art and Visual Technology

My creative pursuits are rather diverse. On the one hand, I have always enjoyed brainstorming for unique names and taglines. I love crafting logos, cartoons, slogans and posters. I love books and song lyrics. I like words and word games. Once in a while, I’ll get engrossed with designing more abstract things like textures and patterns. I have spent many happy hours creating swatches for Illustrator and desktop wallpapers. I think the attention to detail this requires has served me well as an information designer and web developer. It takes self-discipline but the journey is a kick. There’s always something new to learn. Outdoors, I have different interests altogether. I enjoy hiking, biking, exploring trails and capturing wildlife on film. I get tremendous satisfaction out of documenting the transcendent beauty of living things busy just being themselves. Macro photography in particular thrills and fascinates me. Getting Back to Nature: Elements of Design in Insect Photography is a book about finding interesting patterns, compositions and color palettes in everyday photography that can be used as the basis of new designs and art projects. This is a hobby I have always enjoyed but have never written about before. It interests me not only as a design tool, but as a means to revelation or what I think of as the 'Rorschach' effect—the surprising and enlightening things you find whenever you take something out of its original context. As subjects for the book, I used my own macro photography of insects and other animals. I hope the book will inspire readers to look more closely at 'ordinary' things and experiment with their own work, too.
