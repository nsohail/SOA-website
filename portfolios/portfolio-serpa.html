Marcela Serpa

About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (New Media)

Minor Film and Media Studies

Degree BA Art and Visual Technology

My life completely changed when I came to America from Perú at 10 years old. The culture, the language, the people, were all new things that I had to face, but I had faith that one day, I would eventually adapt into my new environment. As I grew older, my main ambition was to inspire people through my experiences and stories that I wanted to share one day. That opportunity arose when I applied art into my life. It was a way to express my emotions and thoughts into a project. Those projects gave me an opportunity to know myself as an artist, and questioned: how could each theme benefit my ambition to inspire others? Therefore, what I do today is to allow others to see life in a new perspective. Whether it deals with hardships or success; I want people to see there’s hope in the lost, and there’s unconditional love when God’s word and teachings are placed in each concept. The delicate value and importance of life is demonstrated ever since the beginning of our body formation. The first stages of the fetus, already state that we are a living thing in this world. Each of us has an opportunity to live with other people, grow, mature, and achieve success through the years. However, not many people get the chance to experience life as they should. Therefore, this concept battles between life and death when there’s a constant loss within children.My work expresses the idea of disappearance and erasure. The young boy acts as an asset to the importance of life. He draws from his memory and his imagination of a living future. However, his life as well as two other children’s, shows the many lives that are lost within our society today. My intention is for the audience to experience a new concept of life and how valuable it is. It is to place ourselves into the image of the young boy, and wonder what could have happened if a decision was made before their birth. A life that was never purposely created but instead taken away.
