Sezgi Dersan

About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (Graphic Design)

Degree BFA Art and Visual Technology

Design has been a passion of mine since I was 13 years old. I have started my design studies with industrial design in Istanbul, Turkey, where I am originally from. Following unforeseen circumstances, I moved to the United States before finishing my degree. Here, I made a decision to start over and study graphic design. I believe that having experience in two different design fields enables me to have a broad understanding of design as a field, and also encourages me to seek intersection points between product design and graphic design. I believe that design has a mission to alleviate the quality of life of the consumer. In my design process, I seek an analytical approach that will enable me to create designs that fulfill their functions, but that are also balanced, memorable, and pleasant to look at. I believe in the intelligence of the consumer, and like to “tickle” their visual perception by using visual puns and playful designs where appropriate. Within the graphic design field, the most attractive studies for me were typography and branding. I look forward to improving myself in these areas, and incorporate hand lettering and calligraphy into my repertoire. One of my goals after graduation is to enter the handmade wedding invitation market, hopefully with my handwritten calligraphy invitations. I have acquired a good collection of resources for this goal, which even includes a Victorian handwriting manual from 1896! One of my biggest long-term goals is to be able to incorporate product design into my graphic design career, making the most of the education I have received so far. One of my other long-term goals is to break into typeface design.In Letter My Hand, I document the process of three hand-lettering projects in an instructional way. The purpose is to introduce the reader to hand lettering, and build initial confidence to help the reader improve on their newly-acquired skills later. Letter My Hand is also a “live” process of book creation, the written content was created as the visual content evolved. With this project, I wish to share both my own works and my process with the reade.
