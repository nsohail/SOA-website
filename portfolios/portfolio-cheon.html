About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (Graphic Design)

Degree BA Art and Visual Technology

I was born in a small town in the Republic of Korea and never left the area until I came to the United States at age 17. It was a quiet place to live and I was used to the peaceful environment that surrounded me. When I was young, I spent most of my time drawing objects that I saw and the objects that I wanted to see. I learned to be creative during that time. However, as I grew older, I lost my connection to the drawings I did and I could not figure out what I wanted to do. I got into college aiming for a degree in the School of Management, which was not my original choice. After switching majors a few times, one AVT class got enough interest from me to make me become an AVT student. Since then, I have been able to figure out what I want to do and what I like to do.

Wings in My Lens is a photo guide book to be used as a guide for people who are interested in the topic of civil aviation and want to learn more about airliners. The book contains information about different aircraft types and experiences about taking photographs of them. The audience will be able to understand the information about the different types of airliners that I have photographed by looking at the photographs, illustrations and written descriptions. Illustrations and infographics of airliners support the photographs by providing related information about them while notes of my personal experiences will add a little more interest to the content of the book. Most of the book's content is made from my pure interest towards the topic. In conclusion, the book will be a simple photo guide book that's easy to understand for the people who are interested in civil aviation.
