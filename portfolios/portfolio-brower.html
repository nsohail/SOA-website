About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (Drawing)

Minor Art History

Degree BA Art and Visual Technology

I am an introverted idealist. I see art as the most genuine form of self-expression and interaction. It helps me to communicate who I am and what I believe in without having to use words. I often seen the world in color and give vision to my thoughts through art. Our imagination is one of the most powerful tools we possess. My goal is to alter reality and in turn transform the viewer’s perception in hopes that they envision life a little bit differently. My illustrations are always created in color, whether through ink or colored pencil. Color brings my offbeat creations into a perceptible existence. As chaotic as my concept may seem, my compositions are persistently balanced and symmetrical. This symmetry provides structure to my unstructured world. Most of my themes are inspired in some way by nature. I tend to see the beauty and importance in things that are quite often overlooked. Whether it is fungi or sea creatures, I take my inspiration and put it in a dream-like context. This series in particular focuses on four environmental issues that correlate with a specific natural element, such as air, earth, fire and water. Within these scenes, I’ve created my own personifications of “Mother Nature” that are trying to protect the environment and prevent its pollution. It puts a twist on this current issue and places it in an unlikely environment. I’m compelled by the possibilities of our imagination and this concept of enthralling the viewer. Ultimately, I’m aiming to stir up the child-like imagination we all still hold deeply.
