Design Portfolio

In social moments—whether at parties, concerts, or the course of a walk—the subjects of these portraits were recorded with a cellphone camera. The resulting photographs and their accompanying memories form the basis of these paintings.

In these works, the relationship between painter and painting is mediated and defined by the existing relationship between painter and model.

The original photographs are not directly translated onto the surface. Alterations of color, form, and motion build an emotional new reality in dialogue with the memory of the past.

Through gesture, color, and composition, the paint expresses its own story. The works are not premeditated, but evolve through a process of blank observation and reaction. The surfaces of these images become emotive landscapes which seek to express the essence of each subject.

The paintings represent the emotional and physical viewpoint of the painter.Therefore, these works ultimately become acts of indirect self-portrait. Each work becomes a record of a connection, whether brief or intimate, between the painter and another.
