About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (Graphic Design)

Degree BFA Art and Visual Technology

I’m originally from Morocco and I speak English, Arabic and French fluently. There are three adjectives that most accurately describe me: outspoken, logical and compassionate. These qualities influence how I interact with people, what hobbies I enjoy, and how I view situations in my life. Although, I tend to surround myself with people with similar qualities, I also find value in people who are very different from me. I like to make things look excellent. Design has been my hobby for over ten years now. Besides design, I love fashion. Fashion has always been a big influence on me, especially when I was a little girl. My senior book—titled Kaftan “the symbol of continuity”: The Moroccan Tradition Wedding Ceremony— has taken both of these creative fields and combines them into something remarkable and breathtaking. As a Moroccan woman, I was brought up to celebrate my cultural traditions. My family has always regarded taking part in wedding ceremonies as a way to honor the extended family. The Moroccan traditional wedding ceremony is a vital part of Moroccan culture. The reason I say that, is because learning more about Moroccan weddings is to learn more about Morocco. Women in general wear Moroccan kaftan; the word kaftan in Morocco is commonly used for one-piece dress. They are composed of two or more pieces plus a belt. A Kaftan can be worn on both casual and extremely formal occasions, depending on the materials used. I personally believe that young people have to preserve their cultural traditions for the next generation and getting married in a traditional ceremony as a way of honoring one’s cultural traditions. My goal of designing this book and writing about it, is that my audience will have a better knowledge of how the Moroccan ceremonies are conducted and also learn about Moroccan culture.
