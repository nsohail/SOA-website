About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (Graphic Design)

Degree BA Art and Visual Technology

I’m an exchange student from China, and I have been at George Mason University for three years. I learned a lot during this period and I feel so lucky to be here. It has already been eight years since I got in touch with art. The first time, I had no interest in art at all. However, my mom forced me to study drawing. At that moment, I found everything about art fascinating, so I fell in love with it. Then, I decided to study design in college. I think the most important thing I have learned after eight years is observation. As a designer, our life is full of inspirations, and observation is an extremely important tool to find those inspirations. As a result, my motto is “open your eyes, and live your life.” Chinese say eating is the paramount necessity of people, just like firmament. In China, eating is a really essential part of people’s life. Its eating culture is really rich, colorful and fascinating. Nowadays, more and more Americans are interested in Chinese culture, especially Chinese cuisine. They love to eat hot sour soup, orange chicken, beef with broccoli and so on. After they finish their dishes, they love to open the fortune cookie to check their luck for fun. However, I bet you don’t know you can never find those things in China. As a result, there are a lot of differences between American Chinese food and real Chinese food. My book introduces a fascinating part of Chinese culture (food) to Americans, lets you understand more about Chinese food, and lets you fall in love with it. This book includes eight distinct Chinese regional cuisines, which are Chuan, Cantonese, Lu, Min, Su, Xiang, Hui, and Zhe. The book also includes some recipes of famous regional Chinese dishes, some popular American Chinese food, and interesting background facts about chopsticks at the end. I hope you can find the joy of cooking.
