I was born and brought up in India, a country rich and vibrant in art. My design style is clean and orthodox. I want to become a creative marketing manager for a fashion-publishing house. Other than being passionate about art, I have over 10 years of training in Indian freestyle music.

My book, The Wedding Story: A Perfect Guide for an Indian Bride is a guidebook for brides who want to know more about the wedding ceremonies, the significance of each, and what they can do to make the most important day of their lives a little more personal. An Indian wedding is a grand, colorful, week-long and very lavish occasion. Family and friends get together to celebrate the couple’s most important day. The groom’s family and the bride’s family get together to decide and plan each one of the six functions. I got the idea for this book when my aunt and uncle were planning my cousin’s wedding, instead of her planning it herself. I thought to myself, “Isn’t the bride supposed to plan how she wants to get married?” My mom then explained to me that in India, culturally and traditionally, it is the parents who plan the wedding because they find it their responsibility to do all they can to make the wedding an extravagant affair, so that they can send their daughter off to her new house happily. But that wasn’t a good enough explanation for me. I understood that it was the parents who decided the caterer, the vendors and the location, but the bride should get the chance to pick her outfits, her makeup artist and other trivial details that concern her. I decided to make a book that would be handy for the brides in deciding the things they want to do for their wedding.
