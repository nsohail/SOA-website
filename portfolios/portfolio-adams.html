William Adams

About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (Painting)

Degree BFA Art and Visual Technology

The work that I have been doing is inspired by Jackson Pollock and abstract expressionism. Throughout my time at Mason, I began to feel as if each painting was just an assignment and that the process of painting became monotonous. Wanting to revitalize my passion for art, I turned to AbEx. The physical demand of this style, and the flow of each stroke from my arm made me fall in love immediately.

This piece is a combination of a painting and a sculpture. I have always been attracted to landscapes; looking at the hills, the trees, the mountains and rocks. I wanted to bring in the randomness of nature and combine it with the flow of the paint. Building up layers of material, (paper, glass, and canvas), the build-up of paint made the process very natural. Throughout the creation, the piece felt as if I was constructing my own world and building up layers of rock and sediments. A natural process that occurred during the creation for this piece was the effect of gravity on the paint; as layers of material were building up, the paint began to run down the slopes of the material. It began to mix with other materials creating a unique pattern that cannot be made by the human hand. This created an even greater sensation of nature within the work; I would make my paint strokes, but gravity had the final input on the outcome of the lines and color.

The painting is placed on a table that is five feet high for a different experience to the viewer. By moving the canvas from the wall to a table, it changes the entire complexity of the artwork. The painting itself is still visible as you move between each hill. Something that I love most about this piece is that from almost any angle, the artwork transforms from what you looked at before. Because of this, there are now an endless amount of images that you can see from the work. I hope you all enjoy this piece as much as I enjoyed creating it. Thank you for your time.
