Ogo Batzorig

About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (Graphic Design)

Degree BFA Art and Visual Technology

I am a passionate graphic designer, student leader, pattern fanatic, and idea generator who likes making friends with like-minded people. I am originally from Mongolia and grew up in Ulaanbaatar. I moved to the USA in 2005 and have been living in Virginia ever since. I am inspired by Scandinavian design and like creating clean, minimal and modern design that is well thought out to meet the users’ needs. I enjoy taking on complex problems; analyze its content in depth, and work to produce a design that is content driven and visually stunning. I plan to continue my career path in the web and UX design world. During my academic years, I have been active in AIGA–Mason Student Group, holding a leadership position of vice president. I head a team of passionate design students to facilitate networking events, design contests, and critique sessions as well as encourage them to develop skills and exchange creative ideas outside of the classroom. I work at the Students Involvement Office of George Mason University as a graphic designer, creating identities for sub-brands and events through logo design, illustration, brochures, posters, and marketing campaigns for university events and organizations. Visually appealing designs resulted in an increase in student participation of events. When I have free time, I like to spend time outdoors playing with my dog Yuki. I also like to browse through design magazines and websites to keep up with the latest styles and trends. My book is designed and created to inform the general public about Mongolian history through the exploration of a represented woman. Mongolia was once the largest of the Khans’ empire, stretching from China to Europe and the Middle East. Within its rich history, there are many warriors and heroic figures, not just male, but female figures, in The Golden Family of Chenggis Khan. The Princess Khutulun, Chenggis Khan’s great-great-granddaughter, is one of the heroic women, noted for her skills in wrestling, archery and horseback riding. A Venetian explorer, Marco Polo, highlighted her strength and bravery, and described her as a fearless warrior. She is described to have been a beautiful and strong woman. Her radiant beauty attracted many suitors across the empire. However, Khutulun never felt the need of a husband. Her only desire was to accompany her father Qaidu in battles and become a leader of the Mongol army.
