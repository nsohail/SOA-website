Masie Chong

About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (Graphic Design)

Degree BFA Art and Visual Technology

I like to think of myself as a creative sleeping artist. An explorer of art mediums, I started with drawing and crafts, stumbled into photography, and am currently in my niche of graphic design. I love that there are no boundaries in art and that anything is possible if I put my mind to it. My favorite aspect about my personal work is being able to incorporate my family and friends into it, making my pieces more personal and meaningful to me. I do art and design just because of my passion for it and it makes me happy. My future dream job would be to travel all over Asia and live in the culture. I am working on learning and perfecting Chinese, Japanese, and Korean during my spare time in preparation for the trip abroad someday. The most exciting activity I look forward to is finally being able to eat all the delicious food I see on television. And so the trips to the gym begin. Even though I am excited for the trip abroad someday, I am perfectly content with staying at home because of my mom’s delicious cooking. Most of my happy childhood memories are of me eating her food and bonding with my family over meals. I decided to create this cookbook as a tribute to her. My mom was going to open a restaurant at one point because everyone loves her cooking that much, but she didn’t go through with it in the end. However this worked out for my siblings and I because we were, and still are, the lucky ones who get to eat delicious dishes everyday. It is because of her careful attention to our meals, like making sure she incorporates enough vegetables for the day, that we were able to grow up with little worries about our health. The most impressive part of my mom’s cooking is that she makes food an art, on a budget. Most of my mom’s meals are under $10 but we never feel the budget with her food. My family is proud to say our mom can make delicious food with so little money. With simple ingredients, and her natural instincts, my mom creates food that I see as artistic creations. Being able to eat such beautiful food is a privilege and makes me very happy.
