Design Portfolio

Art is the type of creative act that takes tenacity as well as passion. For me, art is intimate. We all have a drive to make things ‘special’, but there is a difference between what is special and what is art. Good art is vulnerable, and throughout my body of work I try to expose as many facets of myself as I can. My art extends across many different mediums and modes of process, and while I’m a student of design, I do not limit myself to the screen of a computer. Thematically, the core of my work converges on the concept of identity. As a society, we tend to put ourselves in boxes, and there is a growing obsession to put a label on those boxes, even though people resist compartmentalization. I focus on this dissonance—the difference between who people express themselves to be and how they are perceived.

My senior project aims to study the identity we lend to culture, and the potential discourse between the relation of one culture and the next. When we impose our own stereotypes of certain cultures onto all aspects of that culture, including their language, there is dissonance. Throughout this series, the juxtaposition between what someone sees and what someone hears will be subjective. Depending on the viewers’ internal judgments to the external stimulation, this subjectivity may lead to discomfort, reassurance, and possibly, enlightenment.
