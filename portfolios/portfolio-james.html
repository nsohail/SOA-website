About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (Graphic Design)

Degree BFA Art and Visual Technology

Graphic design is arguably the only art the average person sees on a daily basis, which makes it our duty to create works that do not take our omnipresence for granted. With the competition between designers increasing as the popularity of design programs becomes more mainstream, I have devoted myself not only to the art of graphic design but also the art of self-criticism. In both my professional and personal lives, my dedication and focus have become my reputation.

My passion for design is concentrated in advertising and brand management but spills over into the world of editorial design. I use space, color, and text to create visually interesting work that lead my viewers through space but also use their subconscious aesthetic desires to reinforce their connection with the work. The pieces I create do not have spotlights or screaming colors because they do not need them. There is subtlety in my work so that anyone unfamiliar with design will only notice that it “just feels right.” My passion for self-improvement extends beyond my professional life. For the past two years, I have dedicated myself to changing my lifestyle from that of a couch potato into that of a female bodybuilder. Training at the gym has not only vastly improved my physical health, but my mental health as well. Because of the impact weight lifting has had on my life, I want to share the knowledge I have gained with other women who may not have considered weightlifting as a proper fitness lifestyle for the average person. There are many misconceptions about female bodybuilding that seem to deter women from wanting to try training with weights at the gym. My goal is not only to educate women about the reality of the world of female bodybuilding, but also to motivate them to try weightlifting for themselves. I hope to help create a foundation on which women looking for new routines or a positive change can build a new lifestyle for themselves. A lifestyle change is never easy, but Iron Girls gives readers an overview of the benefits of bodybuilding and what this fitness lifestyle requires so that they can make an informed decision about whether weightlifting is a choice that would suit their life and needs.
