About Me

Major Art and Visual Technology (Graphic Design)

Degree BA Art and Visual Technology

I was born in South Korea in 1990 to one of the most traditional yet typical families. I was never taught nor guided to become an artist or designer. The fundamental artistic sense and interest was naturally given. However, my brother and grandfather who both have great talent in art influenced me. It was around 2003 that I encountered my first career goal. It was an art collections book done by one of the finest concept artists in South Korea, Hyung-Tae Kim. In 2006, I came to America as an international student and went to Fork Union Military Academy, which does not offer an art program. Then I enrolled at George Mason University as a business student at first but quickly lost interest. This was the first time I stood up and fought for my true interest. I have now started to learn drawing, art, and graphic design at GMU to become a better concept artist. A wise man once said, “An ultimate level of martial arts is like a masterpiece drawn on canvas.” There are different kinds of self-defense skills or hand to hand combat styles, but not all of them are considered martial arts. We call this well designed, composed, organized, refined, and yet classical hand to hand combat style as an ‘Art’. Not all art has history. But memorable art pieces, jewelry, architecture, art figures, and masterpieces have fascinating history just like the martial arts. My book doesn’t cover every style and skill in martial arts, but it drowns you deep into the sea of its history and stories. Legends, fairy tales, actual history... each story tells you how a particular martial arts formed, established, and was inherited throughout history and its culture. I really hope my illustrations and design helps you to start this great journey..
