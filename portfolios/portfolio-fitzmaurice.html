Dane Fitzmaurice

About Me

Majors Art and Visual Technology (Painting) + Art and Visual Technology (Computer Game Design)

Degree BFA Art and Visual Technology

This series of paintings imagines my life as a video game, and considers how concept art for this game would look like. Similar to game concept art, each painting in this series attempts to communicate key ideas and defining elements of my life. Recognizable imagery is used to ground the central idea of each painting, while the overall arrangement and aesthetic depiction seeks to bind everything together in a conceptual space that instills the composition with a thematic and emotional charge. The intention behind this mode of portrayal is to give form to these conceptual framings of my life story, and to communicate the personal value I find in them.

As an active participant in the culture of playing and creating video games, I have a growing concern that my peers in this medium have a propensity to lose sight of the value and significance of the experiences that define their own lives, and instead favor their virtual counterparts. So while my work describes ideas specific to myself, I hope that the viewer is enticed into momentarily considering similar perspectives. By doing so, I want them to reflect upon the people, places, and events that have influenced their own lives, and the significance that they hold. Perhaps this will lead them to wonder, as I have, if the greatest story that we each can come to know, or the greatest "game” that can be played, is the one that describes our own journey through life.

I am pursuing a double-BFA in both Art and Visual Technology (with a concentration in painting) and Computer Game Design. On the rare occasions that I am not neck-deep in schoolwork, I enjoy painting (both on the canvas and on the computer), playing video games with friends, and star gazing. I’m also currently the lead artist of a game design company of founded by GMU students and alumni, Bruxe Studios.
